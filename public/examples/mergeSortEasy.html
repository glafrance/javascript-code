Merge sort algorithms divide an array of items to be sorted into two temporary arrays, recursively apply the merge sort on the two temporary arrays, and then merge the two sorted temporary arrays into the final sorted array.

Consider using a merge sort with this array: [ 2, 9, 5, 4, 8, 1, 6, 7 ]

First the merge sort divides the array into two temporary arrays: [ 2, 9, 5, 4 ] and [ 8, 1, 6, 7 ]

Then the merge sort is recursively applied to both of these arrays until they are sorted: [ 2, 4, 5, 9 ] and [ 1, 6, 7, 8 ]

And finally, the two sorted temporary arrays are merged to the final, sorted array: [ 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 ]

To more clearly understand how merge sort operates, we will look at each step.

Starting array: [ 2, 9, 5, 4, 8, 1, 6, 7 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 2, 9, 5, 4 ] and [ 8, 1, 6, 7 ]

Recursively merge sort the first array: [ 2, 9, 5, 4 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 2, 9 ] and [ 5, 4 ]

Recursively merge sort the first array: [ 2, 9 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 2 ] and [ 9 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 2 ] and [ 9 ] become [ 2, 9 ] (they were already in sorted order)

Recursively merge sort the second array: [ 5, 4 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 5 ] and [ 4 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 5 ] and [ 4 ] become [ 4, 5 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 2, 9 ] and [ 4, 5 ] become [ 2, 4, 5, 9 ]

Recursively merge sort the second array: [ 8, 1, 6, 7 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 8, 1 ] and [ 6, 7 ]

Recursively merge sort the first array: [ 8, 1 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 8 ] and [ 1 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 8 ] and [ 1 ] become [ 1, 8 ]

Recursively merge sort the second array: [ 6, 7 ]

Two temporary arrays: [ 6 ] and [ 7 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 6 ] and [ 7 ] become [ 6, 7 ] (they were already in sorted order)

Merge these two in sorted order [ 1, 8 ] and [ 6, 7 ] become [ 1, 6, 7, 8 ]

Merge these two in sorted order [ 2, 4, 5, 9 ] and [ 1, 6, 7, 8 ] become [ 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 ]
